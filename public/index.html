01
Expression-Gated Consciousness
A formal framework for the mechanism of expressive suppression
Psychology Live Study NYU Published

EGC proposes that psychological transparency — the clarity of inner experience — is gated by resistance to expression. The framework introduces a novel resistance term into a consciousness equation and identifies three distinct expression types that emerge under evaluative pressure. Co-authored with Brandyn Leonard.

The study is live and growing. In active dialogue with Dr. Joshua Aronson (NYU), co-author of the landmark 1995 Steele & Aronson stereotype threat paper — one of the most replicated findings in social psychology. The preprint is published. The data strengthens with every new participant.

02
LOLM — Language Model Architecture
Building language intelligence from the ground up
AI/ML PyTorch TPU Research

Designing and training a novel language model architecture from scratch — moving beyond standard Transformer-only approaches. Built a multi-component hybrid system that consistently outperforms conventional baselines across multiple training runs.

Key result at 1.57B parameters: 33.2 PPL vs 39.1 for a matched decoder-only baseline — a 15.1% improvement with identical training compute. Ablation: removing either path catastrophically collapses performance, proving the architecture discovers a fundamentally different decomposition than Transformers or SSMs alone.

Trained models from 20M to 1.57B parameters. Accepted into Google's TPU Research Cloud (320 chips, active). Provisional patent filed March 2026 (USPTO). Applying to accelerators and compute grants to scale to 7B–10B. Co-developing with Brandyn Leonard. Paper forthcoming.

Architecture details proprietary.
